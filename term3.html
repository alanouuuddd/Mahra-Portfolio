Term 3

AI & Advanced Cybersecurity

Exploring how Artificial Intelligence is reshaping cybersecurity — from threat detection to fake media, online fraud, and website risk analysis.

In Term 3, I explored the cutting edge of cybersecurity — how AI is used both by defenders (to detect threats) and attackers (to create deepfakes and carry out fraud). I also studied how internet tools analyze websites for safety and played cybersecurity simulation games to reinforce my skills.

Topics Covered

🤖
Topic 01

AI-Powered Threat Detection Systems

AI can analyze millions of events per second to identify patterns that indicate a cyberattack — something impossible for humans to do manually. Machine learning models are trained on past attacks to recognize and block new threats in real time.

How it works: AI analyzes login times, locations, typing speed, and network traffic. If something unusual is detected, it alerts security teams or automatically blocks the action.
Machine Learning Behavioral Analysis SIEM Systems
💰
Topic 02

Money Threats, Scams & Online Fraud

Online financial fraud is one of the fastest-growing forms of cybercrime. Attackers use increasingly sophisticated methods to steal money and financial data from individuals and businesses.

Common Types:
  • Phishing — fake emails/sites stealing banking credentials
  • Vishing — phone call scams pretending to be banks
  • Smishing — SMS-based fraud with fake links
  • Cryptocurrency Scams — fake investment platforms
🎞
Topic 03

Detecting Fake Media – Images, Videos & Audio

AI can now generate incredibly realistic fake images, videos (deepfakes), and audio. This poses major risks to trust, security, and democracy. Fortunately, other AI tools are being developed to detect this fake content.

How to Spot Fakes: Look for unnatural eye blinking, blurry edges around hair, inconsistent lighting, distorted audio sync, and unnatural facial expressions in videos.

Detection Methods

👀
Visual Artifacts

Pixel inconsistencies & blur edges

🎤
Audio Analysis

Unnatural tone & frequency patterns

🧠
AI Detection Tools

Deepware, Microsoft Video Authenticator

🔍
Reverse Search

Trace image origin using Google Images

🎮
Topic 04

AI & Cybersecurity Games – Learning Through Simulation

Cybersecurity games and simulations are interactive ways to practice real-world skills in a safe environment. From CTF challenges to AI-powered simulators, these tools helped me apply what I learned.

Simulations I Used: Online phishing simulators, network attack/defense games, and AI-powered cyber range tools that simulate real attack scenarios.
CTF Challenges Cyber Range Gamified Learning
🌐
Topic 05

How the Internet Analyzes Websites & Detects Risks

There are tools and systems that automatically scan websites for security vulnerabilities, malicious code, and suspicious behavior. This protects users before they even visit a site.

Tools Used: Google Safe Browsing, VirusTotal, SSL certificate checkers, WHOIS lookup, and browser security warnings.
SSL / HTTPS VirusTotal Safe Browsing API

Real-Life Connections

How cybersecurity protects the systems we depend on every day

🏠

Banks & Online Payments

Banks use multi-factor authentication, AI fraud detection, and end-to-end encryption to protect financial transactions and customer accounts.

🏫

Schools & Education

Schools protect student records with secure login portals, content filters, and staff training to prevent data breaches and unauthorized access.

🏭

UAE Government Systems

The UAE uses advanced cybersecurity infrastructure including the UAE-CERT agency, strict cybercrime laws, and national ID authentication for e-government services.

📱

Social Media Protection

Platforms use AI to detect fake accounts, hate speech, phishing links, and impersonation — protecting billions of users from harm.

🔌

Smart Devices & IoT

Smart home devices, wearables, and industrial sensors require regular updates, strong default passwords, and network isolation to stay secure.

💡 Term 3 Key Takeaway

AI is both a powerful defense tool and a dangerous weapon in the wrong hands. Understanding how AI is used to detect threats, generate fake media, and analyze risks gives us the knowledge to stay ahead. Cybersecurity is no longer just a technical field — it is essential for every person, every business, and every government in the modern world.
