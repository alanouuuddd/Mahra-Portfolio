Term 1

Cybersecurity Awareness

Exploring the foundations of online safety, digital identity, and understanding cyber threats in everyday life.

In Term 1, I learned the basics of cybersecurity — what it means to be safe online, how attacks like phishing work, what your digital footprint reveals about you, and how to protect networks and schools from cyber threats.

Topics Covered

🔒
Topic 01

Cybersecurity Awareness

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting computers, networks, and data from unauthorized access or attacks. Being aware means knowing the types of threats that exist and how to avoid them.

Key Threats: Malware, Ransomware, Social Engineering, Phishing, DDoS Attacks
🎣
Topic 02

Fake Emails & Phishing Protection

Phishing is when attackers send fake emails pretending to be a trusted source (bank, school, company) to trick you into giving personal information or clicking malicious links.

How to Spot: Check sender email carefully, avoid suspicious links, look for spelling errors, never share passwords via email.
Spoofed Emails Fake Login Pages Social Engineering
▲
Topic 03

Information Security – Triangle of Security (CIA)

The CIA Triad is the foundation of information security. Hover over each corner of the triangle to explore each principle in detail.

🔒
Confidentiality

Only authorized users can access sensitive information.

Methods: Encryption, Access Controls, MFA, Data Classification
✅
Integrity

Data must remain accurate and unaltered at all times.

Methods: Checksums, Digital Signatures, Hashing, Audit Logs
⚡
Availability

Systems and data must be accessible when needed.

Methods: Redundancy, Backups, Load Balancing, DDoS Protection
🖤
Topic 04

Digital Footprint & Online Behavior

Your digital footprint is the trail of data you leave behind when using the internet — posts, searches, purchases, and even location data.

Active Footprint: Things you choose to share (posts, emails, sign-ups)
Passive Footprint: Data collected without you knowing (cookies, tracking pixels)
Think Before You Post Privacy Settings Matter
📊
Topic 05

Tracking Website Usage – Excel Activity

We used a shared Excel sheet to log every website we visited during class, recording the URL, purpose, and whether it required login. This helped us analyze our own online habits and understand what data we share.

What I Learned: Many sites track login data, cookies, and browsing habits without users realizing it. Being conscious of website usage is an important part of staying safe.
🌐
Topic 06

Cybersecurity in Networks

Networks connect devices together, making communication possible — but they also create entry points for attackers. Securing a network involves multiple layers of protection.

Key Defenses: Firewalls, Encryption (HTTPS/TLS), VPNs, Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS), Network Segmentation
Firewall VPN Encryption
🏫
Topic 07

Cybersecurity in Schools

Schools store sensitive data: student records, grades, personal details, and financial information. Protecting this data is critical. Schools must teach digital safety and enforce strong security policies.

Best Practices for Schools: Strong password policies, content filtering, regular software updates, staff training, and student cybersecurity education

💡 Term 1 Key Takeaway

Cybersecurity starts with awareness. Before learning advanced tools or techniques, the most important step is understanding that threats exist everywhere online — in emails, websites, apps, and even physical devices. By knowing what to look for and developing safe digital habits, anyone can protect themselves and others in the digital world.
