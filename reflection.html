Reflection

My Learning Journey

Looking back on a year of cybersecurity education — what I discovered, the challenges I faced, and how I plan to protect myself and others in the future.

My Reflections

🎓

What Did I Learn This Year?

This year I learned that cybersecurity is not just about computers — it touches every part of our lives. Some of the most important things I learned include:

  • How phishing attacks work and how to recognize fake emails
  • The CIA Triad — Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability
  • How to hash passwords using Python to keep them secure
  • The dangers of the dark web and what data is sold there
  • How AI is used to detect threats and also create deepfakes
  • UAE cybercrime laws and the legal consequences of hacking
  • How IoT devices create new security risks in our homes
⭐

What Was the Most Interesting Topic?

The most interesting topic for me was AI-powered threat detection and deepfake technology. The idea that AI can both protect us AND be used against us is fascinating and a little scary.

Learning how deepfake videos are created and how detection tools try to catch them made me realize how important media literacy is in the modern world. We cannot always trust what we see or hear online anymore.

I also really enjoyed the EV3 Robot Hacking simulation in Term 1 — it was hands-on and showed me that even physical devices connected to a network can be vulnerable.

⚡

What Challenges Did I Face?

Some of the biggest challenges I faced throughout the year were:

  • Python Programming — Understanding the code for password hashing was difficult at first, especially knowing what each function does
  • Technical Vocabulary — Learning new terms like encryption, hashing, salting, VPN, and SIEM took time and practice
  • Understanding the Dark Web — Knowing it exists but not being able to explore it safely made it harder to fully understand
  • Keeping Up with AI Changes — AI in cybersecurity evolves so fast that new tools appear every week, making it hard to stay current

Despite these challenges, I kept researching, asking questions, and practicing — which helped me improve throughout the year.

🛡

How Can I Protect Myself Online in the Future?

Based on everything I learned, here is how I plan to stay safe online:

  • Use strong, unique passwords for every account and a password manager
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on all important accounts
  • Never click links in emails without verifying the sender first
  • Keep all devices and software updated to patch security vulnerabilities
  • Be careful about what I share on social media — protect my digital footprint
  • Use a VPN when connecting to public Wi-Fi networks
  • Verify media before sharing it — use reverse image search or detection tools
  • Stay informed about new cyber threats and UAE cybersecurity laws

Year in Review

A snapshot of my cybersecurity journey across three terms

Term 1

Cybersecurity Awareness

Learned about phishing, the CIA Triad, digital footprints, network security, and experienced hands-on robot hacking with EV3.

Term 2

Systems & Security Foundations

Explored system layers, wrote Python password hashing code, studied dark web risks using the iceberg model, UAE cybercrime laws, and IoT vulnerabilities.

Term 3

AI & Advanced Cybersecurity

Learned how AI detects threats, how deepfakes are made and detected, studied online fraud, and practiced with cybersecurity simulation games.

Now

Portfolio Complete

Compiled all learning into this portfolio — showcasing my understanding, growth, and commitment to cybersecurity as a future professional.

🎓

Final Thoughts

This year in cybersecurity has been one of the most eye-opening experiences of my school life. I started with basic awareness and ended up understanding AI, Python, laws, and real-world threats. I now see the internet differently — I understand the risks, but I also know how to protect myself and others. My goal is to carry these skills into my future career and contribute to creating a safer digital world.

— Mahra Yousif, Grade 12
